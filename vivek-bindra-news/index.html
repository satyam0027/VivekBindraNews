Why We Started Covering Business News

Here's the honest reason.

India is going through a real shift in how people think about work and business. Ten years ago, the default path for most graduates was a government job or a corporate career. Today, a significant portion of young Indians are thinking seriously about starting something of their own.

That shift created a gap. There was a lot of business news — but most of it was written for investors, not for founders. For analysts, not for operators. For readers who already understood the jargon, not for people just figuring things out.

We started Vivek Bindra News to fill that gap. To write about India's business world in a way that's useful to people who are actually in it — not just watching from the outside.

What We Don't Do

We think it's worth being upfront about this.

We don't write sponsored content disguised as editorial. We don't publish a story just because a PR team sent us a press release. We don't chase clicks by making every headline sound more dramatic than the actual story.

We also don't pretend to be neutral when something is clearly good or clearly problematic. Business journalism is more useful when it has a point of view.

Our job is to give you accurate, relevant, and genuinely useful information — and to respect your time.
