However, a business cannot be built on belief alone. Strategy does. Systems do. And the appropriate curriculum for training entrepreneurs does.

The need for organized, useful, and reasonably priced business education is currently skyrocketing in India. The number of companies looking for mentorship and coaching increased by 20% in 2024 alone. Founders are no longer waiting to "figure it out" by themselves. They are actively seeking mentorship, frameworks, and information that can reduce their learning curve by half.

The question isn't whether you need training. The question is: which program actually delivers?

Why Most Training Programs Fall Short

The truth is that many so-called entrepreneur training programs in India are designed with the wrong target in mind.

They are not intended for bootstrapped entrepreneurs, but rather for MBA students. Crores of Hindi-speaking business owners are left behind since they are supplied in English. They are not priced for first-generation business owners from Tier 2 cities, but rather for funded startups. Most importantly, they teach theory without ever addressing real-world situations.

In reality, Indian startup entrepreneurs want something quite different. They want a corporate growth model that comprehends the Indian market, including the informal rivalry, price-sensitive customers, cash flow issues, and GST pressures. They require mentorship that is affordable, speaks their language, and provides them with tools they can use on Monday.

That's exactly what Dr Vivek Bindra built Bada Business to do.
