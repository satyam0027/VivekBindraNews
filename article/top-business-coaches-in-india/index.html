How to Actually Pick a Coach (Without Throwing Money Away)

I've seen founders wasting lakhs on coaching that changed nothing. Here's how to avoid that:

  • Do they know your industry? A tech coach won't help a manufacturing business. Make sure they've worked with companies like yours.
  • What's their actual method? Some use frameworks. Some do mentoring. Some use case studies. Pick what matches how you learn.
  • What's their track record? Don't trust hype. Ask for testimonials. Look for case studies. Did they actually help someone like you?
  • Can you afford it without stress? Coaching in India: ₹20,000 to ₹500,000+ per month. Set a budget first. Don't go over.
  • Do they understand your goals? Your coach should know what you're building — not push their own agenda.

If you're looking for a startup business coach, make sure they've worked with companies at your stage. Early-stage needs different support than scale-ups.

Also, remember that business coaching in India varies wildly in quality. Don't just pick the cheapest. Pick the one who actually delivers.
